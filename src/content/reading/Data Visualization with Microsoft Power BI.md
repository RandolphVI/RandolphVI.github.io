> In general, this boundary between horizontal and vertical placement of columns is around 10 categories. However, depending on the length of labels and the size of the chart, it can be shifted either downward or upward: for 7–8 categories with extended names, you will need to use a bar chart, while for 11–12 categories with short labels, you can stick with a column chart.一般情况下，水平与垂直放置柱状图之间的界限大约在 10 个类别左右。但是，这取决于标签的长度和图表的大小，可以向下或向上移动：对于具有扩展名称的 7-8 个类别，您需要使用条形图，而对于具有短标签的 11-12 个类别，您可以坚持使用柱状图。 

> The charts in Figure 2-12 look good because there are only five sales channels represented, or more precisely, four main ones, while all the others are grouped into the “Other” category. Both types of charts vividly display data within the range of five to seven categories. However, if there are around 10 categories or even more, both charts will appear overloaded (see Figure 2-13).[插图]Figure 2-13. Overloaded pie and donut charts图 2-13. 过载的饼形图和甜甜圈图 

> What to do for seven or more categories? An alternative to pie charts is the relatively new treemap chart. The idea behind it is similar, but instead of cutting a circle into sectors, we divide a rectangle. Because we fill the entire rectangular area with data, it can accommodate 10–12 elements (see Figure 2-14).[插图]Figure 2-14. An alternative to pie charts—the treemap图 2-14. 饼图的替代方案——树状图 

> The rectangular shape also gives us more flexibility than the circle. Depending on the available space on the dashboard, we can stretch it horizontally or vertically. Power BI automatically adjusts the optimal proportions of sectors. However, the treemap also has limitations. On our compass, we set the mark at 12 categories. As an exception, 13–14 may fit, but with 15–20 elements, it will definitely be difficult to discern anything beyond the largest 5–7 categories.
